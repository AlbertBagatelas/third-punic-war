The lead up to the conflict!

The Third Punic War, was one of the most controversial of the three conflicts between Rome and Carthage, and was the result of efforts by Cato the Elder and other hawkish members of the Roman Senate to convince their colleagues that Carthage was a threat to Romeâ€™s dominance in the region. In 149 B.C., after Carthage technically broke its treaty with Rome by declaring war against the neighboring state of Numidia, the Romans sent an army to North Africa, beginning the Third Punic War.

After the War!

With the Romans winning the war it solidified their position in the Mediterranean as the main power.

After the War!

The Romans winning also sent a message to the surronding countries that the Romans were the main power and that no deal should be broken with them.
Modern scholars have advanced several theories as to why Rome was eager for war. These include: a Roman fear of Carthaginian commercial competition, a desire to forestall a wider war which might have broken out with the death of Masinissa, the factional use of Carthage as a political "bogeyman", a greed for glory and loot, and a desire to quash a political system which Rome considered anathema.
